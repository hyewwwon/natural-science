Abstract

Ionic Liquids (ILs) are of many potential applications nowadays, such as electrolytes and lubricants. Commercial lithium-ion batteries’ electrolytes would be consumed by the lithium dendrite[1] to some extend and it may thus pose a threat to the everyday use of batteries. As a potential constituted materials of batteries, this report is mainly aimed to investigate the electrical conductivity of ILs and to discover some of the unique properties of ILs related to its intermolecular forces, including their viscosity and melting point. It found that the electroconductivity of ILs decreases with increased size, whereas the viscosity behaves like a v-shape with larger size and follow almost the same trend to the boiling point. Therefore, there are no absolute theories to support any existing observations and in most cases, it is more reasonable to conclude which factor contribute to overall effect in a larger degree. awesome kms.

Method 3.1 Synthesised of ILs sample The ILs samples are synthesised by reacting 1-methylimidazole with different bromoalkanes (the number of carbons is ranging from 4 to12, excluding 5) followed by anion exchange reaction with KPF6, 1-alkyl-3-methylimidizolium hexafluorophosphates will form at the end of the process. After cooling the mixture to room temperature, the ILs is gained by using the separating funnel to get the bottom layer of mixture. Result and discussion The property of 1-dodecyl-3-methylimidizolium hexafluorophosphate is not discussed here, since it is solid in room temperature due to its strong intermolecular force. 4.1 Trend of electrical conductivity As depicted in Fig.8, the experimental data well fit the previous prediction based on the theory of current’s deduction. Another possible explanation was the different speeds in solution. In constant electric field, the force experience by cations is the same. According to Newton Second law, larger size of cations lead to smaller value of acceleration thereby lower the speed proceed by cations. Furthermore, large size of ions may also make ions hard to move freely about, as there is less room available for them to move or in other words, ions themselves may resist their motion relative to each other. The volume of sample used should be constant for each ILs, otherwise the area is not controlled in the equation R=pL/A. This would help to increase the reliability of electroconductivity comparison between similar sizes of ILs, because the current is in a small scale (uA). Fig.8 Fig.9 4.2 Trend of viscosity The data shown on Fig.9 almost shares a similar pattern with their boiling point. However, 1-butyl-3- methylimidazolium hexafluorophosphate is almost stationary. One possible explanation would be its strong electrostatic force within molecules and its strong interaction with particles of ruler in micro-state. Since the viscosity of ILs varies, several trials for rough measurement for time is necessary. Only when the rough data are within an acceptable range (related to accuracy calculation), should the accurate data start to measure. Besides, the oil-like ILs layers on the ruler should also be cleaned off, as they are very slipper which can significantly affect the data measured. However, it is still hard to ensure all the the data is gained at the same condition, i.e.difficult to determine whether the ruler is clean and the cellulose of tissue may left on ruler during drying process due to limited equipment. 4.3 Other improvements for the experiment Sand bath is to ensure a thorough heating. However, because of the relative high specific heat capacity of sand, it is time consuming to wait for its temperature to rise above he activation temperature. Therefore, it is better to set the initial temperature of heating mantle much more higher than the activation temperature to shorten the time needed. Then, the time measurement for heating is first started when there are obvious water drops appear at the surface of the round bottom flask, which is to ensure a complete reaction of mixture.
